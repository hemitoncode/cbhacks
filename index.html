02 — About

Where legends
are forged.

Castlebrooke Hacks '26 is Brampton's premier high-school hackathon — two full days of building, learning, and competing alongside the sharpest student minds in Ontario.

Hosted at Castlebrooke Secondary School, this is a daytime in-person event with no overnight stays. Show up, build something incredible, and head home with memories (and prizes) that last.

Whether you're a seasoned hacker or picking up a keyboard for the first time, there's a team, a track, and a prize with your name on it. Come hungry. Leave legendary.

04 — Workshops

Level up your
craft.

Two workshops run during the event — designed to give every hacker, beginner to advanced, sharper tools and sharper thinking.

WS — 01 · ADVANCED
Open Code:
Orchestrating AI Agents

Go beyond single-model prompting. This workshop dives into how to architect multi-agent systems — where models hand off tasks, check each other's work, and run in parallel. Built around real open-source tooling.

Topics covered:
  • ▸ Orchestrating agent teams with Claude & MCP
  • ▸ Building agent-to-agent communication flows
  • ▸ Setting up skills (e.g. frontend design) for richer, more consistent output
  • ▸ Structuring prompts and tool chains that scale
LEVEL  Intermediate – Advanced
WS — 02 · BEGINNER
Code First:
Programming Without AI

AI can write code, but it can't think for you. This workshop teaches the fundamentals of programming — variables, logic, loops, functions — so you actually understand what your AI tools are producing and can guide them intelligently.

Topics covered:
  • ▸ Core programming concepts: variables, types, control flow
  • ▸ Functions, loops, and debugging without a crutch
  • ▸ Reading and understanding code — not just running it
  • ▸ How to leverage AI tools once you know the basics
LEVEL  Complete Beginner

06 — FAQ

Questions,
answered.

Who can participate?

Any student currently enrolled in high school (Grades 9–12) in Ontario. You do not need to attend Castlebrooke Secondary to join.

Do I need a team?

Nope! Solo participants are welcome. You can also form teams of 2–4. We'll have a team formation session at the opening ceremony for those who need one.

What experience do I need?

None at all. We actively encourage beginners. We have workshops, mentors, and a dedicated Best Beginner prize just for first-timers.

Is it free to attend?

Yes — completely free. Meals, drinks, snacks, and swag are all included for the duration of the event. Just register and show up.

What should I bring?

Your laptop, charger, any hardware you plan to use, and a school ID. This is a daytime event — no need to bring sleeping gear or overnight supplies.

How are projects judged?

Projects are evaluated on innovation & creativity, technical complexity, impact & feasibility, and quality of presentation. Judges are industry professionals and alumni.
